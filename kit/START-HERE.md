# Mom Brain: Start Here

You have 40 tabs open in your head right now. The camp form, the dentist, the thing someone needs to bring Friday, whatever's for dinner. This closes them.

Mom Brain is an assistant you set up inside Claude once, in about 15 minutes. It interviews you about your actual family: your picky eaters, your week, your school's rules, the person whose schedule never holds. Then it runs the logistics with you.

- The spirit week email gets caught Sunday afternoon, not Thursday at 9pm.
- "What's for dinner" gets answered once a week instead of five times a day.
- The grocery list writes itself, sorted by your stores in your order.

## What you need

A free Claude account at claude.ai. A paid one is better because Claude can read your Gmail and calendar with your permission, but free works. And 15 quiet minutes. I know. Find them anyway.

## Path A: claude.ai (most of us)

1. Go to claude.ai and sign in.
2. Create a new Project. Name it Mom Brain.
3. Open the file called mom-brain-master-prompt that came with this kit. Copy all of it.
4. Paste it into the Project's instructions. Look for "set project instructions."
5. Start a chat inside the Project and type: set up my mom brain.
6. Answer its questions. It asks one at a time and builds your Family File as you talk.
7. When it hands you the finished Family File, save it into the Project's knowledge so it remembers you next time.

Optional but worth it: in claude.ai settings, connect Gmail and Google Calendar. Then it reads the school emails and writes your calendar itself instead of asking you to paste. Both ways work.

## Path B: Claude Code (for the technical, or your technical person)

1. Copy the claude-code-version folder into your workspace's .claude/skills/ folder and name the folder mom-brain.
2. Open Claude Code and say: set up my mom brain.
3. It interviews you, builds its own memory files, and can run on a schedule so the Sunday brief shows up without you asking.

## The rhythm

Sunday afternoon, open it and type "sunday." You get the week ahead, anything that needs you Monday, and a dinner plan you approve in two minutes.

Monday morning, type "monday." You get the grocery lists and the week on your calendar.

Any other day, paste whatever hits your phone. A party invite, a schedule change, a camp email. Say "add it." Done means it's on the calendar, not in your head.

## Make it yours

This is a template, not a rulebook. If it talks to you wrong, tell it. Shorter. Warmer. Stop suggesting quinoa. It adjusts, and your version should sound like it works for your family, because it does.

One thing I'll hand you from experience: when it asks a question during setup, answer in real words, out loud if that's how you think. The more honest the interview, the fewer things fall on you later.
